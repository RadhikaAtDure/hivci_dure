  • The Global Fund single Concept Note for HIV and TB represents a critical opportunity for financing the HIV response.
  • All levels of WHO stand ready to support the resubmission (as a single concept note) later this year (currently proposed for August)—at present, WHO is supporting the lead HIV consultant (co-funded with UNAIDS) and lead TB consultant (through TBTEAM), among other activities.
  • DRC needs to critically review and revised based on TRP comments—in particular, there is a need to (1) develop a roadmap to how new WHO ARV guidelines will be implemented, (2) ensure seamless collaboration between HIV and TB, (3) address HSS constraints (and consider service availability mapping), and (4) strengthen EMTCT and peds. components.
  • There is also a need to respond to GF concerns related to CCM reform and implementation arrangements.
  • 1.1% HIV prevalence (480,000 people living with HIV). DRC has a generalized epidemic.
  • 31% Antiretroviral therapy coverage.
  • DRC was one of the first countries to acknowledge and respond to HIV in 1983. Rates of HIV infection have stabilized in recent years.
  • DRC is a heavily burdened country for HIV with major implementation challenges. However, some progress has been made in recent years in the areas of treatment scale-up.
  • The President and First Lady have a strong commitment to the HIV response and have been promoting: elimination of mother-to child transmission of HIV; improved reproductive health; and increasing domestic resources.
  • WHO’s support for the national HIV response has been broad-ranging, with a particular focus on eliminating mother-to child transmission of HIV, ART and in reaching Universal Access targets.
